Asami Tsuchiya

Product Designer

I started my career as a furniture designer and have been working in industrial design areas such as furniture, interiors and home goods, as well as graphic/print design for over 15 years. Halfway through my career, I expanded my design expertise to include digital product design, website and mobile app design.

I love to design physical products because my work becomes a part of the user's life; at the same time designing digital products gives me new discoveries about how powerful design can be as a practical solution. I am always excited to learn new digital tools, new methods and new perspectives.

Below, I gathered some things about myself that I was not able to convey to you in my case studies.

What is important and valuable to me

  • Design should always be a solution to a problem
  • Focus to see the essence of the project (= what users need and want to solve)
  • Keep absorbing new skills and knowledge

My strengths

  • Accustomed to seeing a project from the beginning to the end: from researching and creating personas, to delivering products and sharing design
  • Observe the entire project and pay close attention to details so that I can execute the project thoroughly in each step
  • Able to see the project’s business value, such as contribution to sales, given my experience of having worked closely with CEOs of companies in different types of industries
  • Apply my cross discipline experience in industrial and graphic design, depending on the project requirements

Things that motivate me

  • Meeting with users and get feedback directly about a product/design
  • Working with team members to achieve a common vision for a product
  • Recognizing that our products make users' lives better

Things I don’t like

  • Working for someone who doesn’t appreciate creative work
  • Working on a project that forgets about users and focuses on satisfying someone’s ego
  • Having to design without background information of a project/user

My weaknesses and things I want to improve

  • My animation skills in prototyping are not as strong as I want them to be, so I keep learning and updating my knowledge
  • My speaking voice is around 40 dB. It’s true! It may seem that I lack confidence, but actually it is just a low volume. I am focusing on speaking more loudly in conversation
